# Wiki editorial governance runbook

This runbook defines the head-editor workflow for wiki updates produced by orchestration.

## Source-of-truth model

- Canonical wiki pages live under `knowledge/wiki/orchestration/**` and `knowledge/wiki/projects/**`.
- Candidate edits produced during runs are queued under `knowledge/wiki/_proposals/*.md`.
- Candidate edits are **unapproved by default** and include the policy marker:
  - `unapproved_source: true`
  - `caution: "unapproved source, proceed with caution"`

## Lifecycle states

`PROPOSED -> APPROVED -> PROMOTED` or `PROPOSED -> REJECTED`

- `PROPOSED`: generated by control-plane intake/workflow integrations.
- `APPROVED`: explicit editorial approval via control-plane API.
- `PROMOTED`: proposal applied to canonical wiki and compiled catalogs.
- `REJECTED`: explicitly declined; retained for audit/provenance.

## Operational commands

- Validate queue contracts/policy:
  - `make wiki-proposals-check`
- Validate canonical wiki compiler drift:
  - `make wiki-check`
- Promote approved proposals to canonical wiki and recompile:
  - `make wiki-promote`

## API controls

- List proposals:
  - `GET /api/control-plane/wiki/proposals`
- Approve proposal:
  - `POST /api/control-plane/wiki/proposals/{proposal_id}/approve`
- Reject proposal:
  - `POST /api/control-plane/wiki/proposals/{proposal_id}/reject`

Approval/rejection can include `task_id` and `run_id` for dossier/run-event audit persistence.

## Guardrails

- Unapproved proposals must not mutate canonical wiki pages directly.
- Canonical response-control JSON remains compile-driven from approved wiki sources.
- CI enforces both queue validity and compiler drift checks before merge.
